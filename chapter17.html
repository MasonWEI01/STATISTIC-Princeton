Chapter 17: t-Distribution (t分布)

Definitions (定义)

Definition: t-Distribution

If Z is a standard normal random variable, V is a chi-square random variable with n degrees of freedom, and Z and V are independent, then the random variable T = Z/√(V/n) follows a t-distribution with n degrees of freedom, denoted as T ~ t(n).

如果Z是标准正态随机变量，V是自由度为n的卡方随机变量，且Z和V相互独立，则随机变量T = Z/√(V/n)服从自由度为n的t分布，记为T ~ t(n)。

Definition: Student's t-Distribution

Student's t-distribution is another name for the t-distribution, named after William Sealy Gosset who published under the pseudonym "Student".

学生t分布是t分布的另一种称呼，由William Sealy Gosset（笔名"Student"）首次提出。

Definition: t-Test

The t-test is a statistical hypothesis test used to determine if there is a significant difference between the means of two groups, which may be related in certain features.

t检验是一种统计假设检验方法，用于在样本量小且总体标准差未知的情况下，检验样本均值与总体均值的差异是否显著。

Theorems (定理)

Theorem: Symmetry of t-Distribution

The t-distribution is symmetric about the origin, i.e., if T ~ t(n), then -T also follows t(n) distribution.

Theorem: Limiting Distribution of t-Distribution

As the degrees of freedom n approaches infinity, the t(n) distribution approaches the standard normal distribution N(0,1).

Theorem: One-Sample t-Test

If X₁, X₂, ..., Xₙ is an independent and identically distributed random sample from a normal distribution N(μ,σ²), then the statistic T = (X̄ - μ)/(S/√n) follows a t-distribution with n-1 degrees of freedom, where X̄ is the sample mean and S is the sample standard deviation.

Theorem: Two-Sample t-Test

If two independent samples are from normal distributions N(μ₁,σ²) and N(μ₂,σ²) respectively, then under the equal variance assumption, the statistic T = (X̄₁ - X̄₂)/√(S_p²(1/n₁ + 1/n₂)) follows a t-distribution with n₁+n₂-2 degrees of freedom, where S_p² is the pooled sample variance.

Formulas (公式)

Formula: Probability Density Function of t-Distribution
The probability density function of a t-distribution with n degrees of freedom is:
f(t) = Γ((n+1)/2)/(√(nπ)·Γ(n/2))·(1+t²/n)^(-(n+1)/2)
Formula: Expected Value and Variance of t-Distribution
If T ~ t(n) and n > 1, then:
E[T] = 0 (when n > 1)
Var(T) = n/(n-2) (when n > 2)
Formula: Confidence Interval Using t-Distribution
A (1-α) confidence interval for the population mean μ is:
X̄ ± t_(α/2,n-1)·S/√n
where t_(α/2,n-1) is the α/2 quantile of the t-distribution with n-1 degrees of freedom.
Formula: Welch-Satterthwaite Equation
In the two-sample t-test with unequal variances, the approximate degrees of freedom is:
ν ≈ (s₁²/n₁ + s₂²/n₂)²/[(s₁²/n₁)²/(n₁-1) + (s₂²/n₂)²/(n₂-1)]
