Chapter 18: Central Limit Theorem (中心极限定理)

Definitions (定义)

Definition: Central Limit Theorem

The Central Limit Theorem states that, under appropriate conditions, the distribution of the sum of a large number of independent, identically distributed random variables approaches a normal distribution, regardless of the original distribution of these variables.

中心极限定理指出，在适当条件下，大量独立同分布随机变量的均值的分布近似于正态分布，无论这些随机变量本身的分布如何。

Definition: Asymptotic Normality

A sequence of random variables is said to have asymptotic normality if its distribution approaches a normal distribution as the sample size increases.

如果随机变量序列的分布随着样本量增加而趋近于正态分布，则称该序列具有渐近正态性。

Definition: Standardized Random Variable

A standardized random variable is obtained by subtracting its mean and dividing by its standard deviation, resulting in a new random variable with mean 0 and variance 1.

将随机变量减去其均值再除以其标准差得到的新随机变量，其均值为0，方差为1。

Theorems (定理)

Theorem: Classical Central Limit Theorem

Let X₁, X₂, ..., Xₙ be independent and identically distributed random variables with mean μ and variance σ² (0 < σ² < ∞). Then the random variable

Zₙ = (X₁ + X₂ + ... + Xₙ - nμ)/(σ√n)

converges in distribution to the standard normal distribution N(0,1) as n approaches infinity.

Theorem: Lindeberg-Lévy Central Limit Theorem

If {Xₙ} is a sequence of independent and identically distributed random variables with finite mean μ and variance σ², then

√n(X̄ₙ - μ)/σ → N(0,1) (convergence in distribution)

where X̄ₙ = (X₁ + X₂ + ... + Xₙ)/n is the sample mean.

Theorem: Lyapunov Central Limit Theorem

If {Xₙ} is a sequence of independent random variables with means μₙ and variances σₙ², and the Lyapunov condition is satisfied, then the standardized sum converges to the standard normal distribution.

Theorem: Berry-Esseen Theorem

The Berry-Esseen theorem provides an upper bound on the rate of convergence in the central limit theorem, showing that the convergence rate is related to the third moment of the random variables.

Formulas (公式)

Formula: Standard Form of Central Limit Theorem
(Sₙ - nμ)/(σ√n) → N(0,1) (convergence in distribution)
where Sₙ = X₁ + X₂ + ... + Xₙ.
Formula: Asymptotic Distribution of Sample Mean
√n(X̄ - μ) → N(0,σ²) (convergence in distribution)
Formula: Normal Approximation to Binomial Distribution
If X ~ Bin(n,p), then for sufficiently large n,
(X - np)/√(np(1-p)) ≈ N(0,1)
Formula: Normal Approximation to Poisson Distribution
If X ~ Poisson(λ), then for sufficiently large λ,
(X - λ)/√λ ≈ N(0,1)
Formula: Moment Generating Function Method
The moment generating function can be used to prove the central limit theorem by showing that the moment generating function of the standardized sum converges to e^(t²/2).
