Chapter 20: Statistical Estimation (统计估计)

Definitions (定义)

Definition: Parameter Estimation

Parameter estimation is the process of inferring the value of a population parameter based on sample data.

参数估计是根据样本数据推断总体参数值的过程。

Definition: Point Estimation

Point estimation provides a single numerical value as an estimate of a population parameter.

点估计是用样本统计量给出总体参数的单一数值估计。

Definition: Interval Estimation

Interval estimation provides a range of values within which the population parameter is expected to lie with a certain confidence level.

区间估计是给出一个区间，使得总体参数以一定的置信度落在这个区间内。

Definition: Estimator

An estimator is a statistic used to estimate a population parameter, which is a function of the sample data.

估计量是用于估计总体参数的统计量，是样本的函数。

Definition: Unbiased Estimator

An estimator is unbiased if its expected value equals the parameter being estimated.

如果估计量的期望等于被估计的参数，则称该估计量是无偏的。

Definition: Consistent Estimator

An estimator is consistent if it converges in probability to the parameter being estimated as the sample size increases.

如果估计量随着样本量增加而依概率收敛到被估计的参数，则称该估计量是一致的。

Definition: Efficient Estimator

Among all unbiased estimators, the one with the smallest variance is called an efficient estimator.

在所有无偏估计量中，方差最小的估计量称为有效估计量。

Theorems (定理)

Theorem: Properties of Sample Mean

The sample mean X̄ is an unbiased estimator of the population mean μ, and when the sample is from a distribution with finite variance, X̄ is also a consistent estimator of μ.

Theorem: Properties of Sample Variance

The corrected sample variance S² = Σ(Xᵢ - X̄)²/(n-1) is an unbiased estimator of the population variance σ².

Theorem: Properties of Maximum Likelihood Estimator

Under regularity conditions, maximum likelihood estimators are consistent and asymptotically normal.

Theorem: Cramér-Rao Lower Bound

The variance of any unbiased estimator is bounded below by the inverse of the Fisher information.

Formulas (公式)

Formula: Maximum Likelihood Estimation
The maximum likelihood estimate is the value of θ that maximizes the likelihood function L(θ;x₁,x₂,...,xₙ) = f(x₁,x₂,...,xₙ|θ).
Formula: Method of Moments
Method of moments estimation equates sample moments to the corresponding population moments to estimate parameters.
Formula: Confidence Interval
A (1-α) confidence interval for the mean μ is:
X̄ ± z_(α/2)·σ/√n (when σ is known) or X̄ ± t_(α/2,n-1)·S/√n (when σ is unknown)
Formula: Fisher Information
The Fisher information I(θ) is defined as:
I(θ) = E[(∂/∂θ ln f(X|θ))²]
where f(x|θ) is the probability density function.
Formula: Asymptotic Variance
Under regularity conditions, the asymptotic distribution of a maximum likelihood estimator θ̂ is:
√n(θ̂ - θ) → N(0, 1/I(θ))
