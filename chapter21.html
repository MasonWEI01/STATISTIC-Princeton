Chapter 21: Hypothesis Testing (假设检验)

Definitions (定义)

Definition: Statistical Hypothesis

A statistical hypothesis is a statement about a population parameter or distribution that can be tested by statistical tests.

统计假设是关于总体参数或分布的一种陈述，可以被统计检验所验证。

Definition: Null Hypothesis

The null hypothesis (H₀) is the hypothesis that the researcher wishes to test, typically representing "no effect" or "no difference".

原假设（零假设）H₀是研究者希望检验的假设，通常表示"无效应"或"无差异"。

Definition: Alternative Hypothesis

The alternative hypothesis (H₁) is the hypothesis that is contrary to the null hypothesis, representing "there is an effect" or "there is a difference".

备择假设H₁是与原假设相对的假设，表示"有效应"或"有差异"。

Definition: Significance Level

The significance level (α) is the maximum probability of committing a Type I error that the researcher is willing to accept.

显著性水平α是研究者愿意接受的犯第一类错误的最大概率。

Definition: p-value

The p-value is the probability of obtaining the observed result or a more extreme result, assuming that the null hypothesis is true.

p值是在原假设为真的条件下，获得观察到的或更极端结果的概率。

Definition: Test Statistic

A test statistic is a statistic calculated from sample data, used to decide whether to reject the null hypothesis.

检验统计量是从样本数据计算得到的统计量，用于决定是否拒绝原假设。

Definition: Type I Error

A Type I error occurs when the null hypothesis is true but is incorrectly rejected.

第一类错误是指原假设为真但被错误地拒绝的情况。

Definition: Type II Error

A Type II error occurs when the null hypothesis is false but is not rejected.

第二类错误是指原假设为假但未被拒绝的情况。

Theorems (定理)

Theorem: Neyman-Pearson Lemma

In simple hypothesis testing, the most powerful test is based on the likelihood ratio.

Theorem: Power of a Test

The power of a test is the probability of correctly rejecting the null hypothesis when the alternative hypothesis is true, equal to 1 minus the probability of a Type II error.

Theorem: Power of t-test

The power of a t-test depends on the sample size, effect size, and significance level.

Theorem: Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test

The Wilcoxon signed-rank test is a non-parametric test used to test for differences in the median of paired samples.

Formulas (公式)

Formula: z-test Statistic
For testing a mean with known population standard deviation, the z-test statistic is:
z = (X̄ - μ₀)/(σ/√n)
Formula: t-test Statistic
For testing a mean with unknown population standard deviation, the t-test statistic is:
t = (X̄ - μ₀)/(S/√n)
Formula: Chi-Square Goodness-of-Fit Test Statistic
χ² = ∑[(Oᵢ - Eᵢ)²/Eᵢ]
where Oᵢ is the observed frequency and Eᵢ is the expected frequency.
Formula: F-test Statistic
For comparing two population variances, the F-test statistic is:
F = S₁²/S₂²
Formula: Power Calculation
For a z-test, when the alternative hypothesis is μ = μ₁, the power of the test is:
1 - Φ(z_(α) - (μ₁ - μ₀)/(σ/√n))
where Φ is the cumulative distribution function of the standard normal distribution.
