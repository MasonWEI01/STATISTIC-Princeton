Chapter 13: Random Walks (随机游走)

Definitions (定义)

Definition: Random Walk

A random walk is a special type of Markov process where the system moves randomly in some direction at each step.

随机游走是一种特殊的马尔可夫过程，其中系统在每一步随机地向某个方向移动。

Definition: Simple Symmetric Random Walk

A one-dimensional random walk where at each step, the walker moves one unit to the left or right with equal probability.

在一维空间中，每一步等概率地向左或向右移动一个单位距离的随机游走。

Definition: Drunkard's Walk

A drunkard's walk is a colorful description of a simple random walk, representing a drunk person who takes random steps in different directions.

醉汉游走是简单随机游走的一种形象化描述，表示一个醉汉每一步随机地向某个方向移动。

Definition: Absorbing Barrier

Special states in a random walk that, once reached, stop the walking process.

随机游走中的某些状态，一旦到达就停止游走过程的特殊状态。

Definition: Reflecting Barrier

Special states in a random walk that, once reached, reflect the walker back.

随机游走中的某些状态，到达后会被反射回来的特殊状态。

Definition: First Hitting Time

The number of steps needed for a random walk to reach a specific state for the first time.

随机游走首次到达某个特定状态所需的步数。

Definition: Recurrence

A random walk is recurrent if, starting from the origin, it returns to the origin with probability 1.

如果随机游走从原点出发，以概率1最终会返回原点，则称该随机游走是回归的。

Theorems (定理)

Theorem: Recurrence of One-Dimensional Simple Symmetric Random Walk

A one-dimensional simple symmetric random walk is recurrent, meaning that starting from the origin, it will eventually return to the origin with probability 1.

Theorem: Recurrence of Two-Dimensional Simple Symmetric Random Walk

A two-dimensional simple symmetric random walk is also recurrent.

Theorem: Transience of Three-Dimensional and Higher Simple Symmetric Random Walk

A simple symmetric random walk in three or more dimensions is transient, meaning there is a positive probability that it will never return to the origin.

Theorem: Gambler's Ruin Problem

In the gambler's ruin problem, if two players have initial fortunes of i and N-i respectively, and win probabilities of p and q=1-p per round, then the probability that player 1 eventually goes bankrupt is:

- If p=q=1/2, then the bankruptcy probability is (N-i)/N

- If p≠q, then the bankruptcy probability is (1-(p/q)^i)/(1-(p/q)^N)

Theorem: Expected First Hitting Time

For a one-dimensional simple symmetric random walk, the expected first hitting time from position 0 to position n is n².

Formulas (公式)

Formula: Probability of Return to Origin After 2n Steps
For a one-dimensional simple symmetric random walk, the probability of being at the origin after 2n steps is:
P(S₂ₙ = 0) = (2n choose n) / 2^(2n)
Formula: Probability of Being at Position k After n Steps
For a one-dimensional simple symmetric random walk, the probability of being at position k after n steps is:
P(Sₙ = k) = 0 (if n+k is odd)
P(Sₙ = k) = (n choose (n+k)/2) / 2^n (if n+k is even)
